# Training the Idea Muscle
## Summary
Profile-style essay on idea generation and creative execution, centered on Riley Walz's high-output project workflow and "build first, refine later" mindset.

## Key Points
- Argues that frequent execution strengthens idea quality over time.
- Emphasizes reducing self-rejection and acting on imperfect concepts quickly.
- Uses concrete examples of viral side projects and unconventional experimentation.
- Contrasts skill acquisition-through-doing with overplanning.
- Frames creative momentum as a compounding asset for builders.
